The Problem

Most contractors are building the plane while flying it.

Every week without a system costs you. Not in theory — in real money, real time, and real jobs that should have been yours.

01

Your techs don't know what to say

Without scripts and communication protocols, every service call depends on whoever shows up that day. Some techs close. Some don't. Nobody knows why.

02

You're pricing by gut feel

If your price doesn't include overhead allocation, fully burdened labor rate, and a deliberate margin target, you're not running a business. You're running a charity with trucks.

03

Dispatch is a daily fire drill

No zone structure. No brief protocol. No job lifecycle tracking. The right tech showing up to the right job at the right time is luck, not a system.

04

Every hire costs more than you planned

Onboarding is inconsistent. Training is on-the-job. You don't know what a new hire actually costs per hour, and you have no way to measure if they're profitable.

05

Money's moving but you don't know where

Invoices aging past 30 days. No reserve calculation. No gross margin floor. Revenue is strong, but the account doesn't reflect it, and you can't explain why.

06

You're the system

When you're the only person who knows how everything works, nothing scales. Every vacation is a crisis. Every key employee who quits takes institutional knowledge with them.
